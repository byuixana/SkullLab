2D Skull Anatomy

Click on the small "+" icon to the right of the 2D Skull Anatomy menu tab. This will open up a menu with all the bones of the skull that you are learning. As you click on the bones, you will see 2D images of a skull pull up with highlights on the structures you are studying. Note that many of the images have buttons at the top that allow you to see different views or information about that structure. Also, notice that many of the bones have yet an other "+" symbol which allows you to drop down a deeper menu to study pictures of more structures on each bone. Finally, notice that as you click throud the structures on the mnenu, some of them will have an icon of a speaker. If you click on these you can hear the structures pronounced.

As you go through the images in this section, you will notice that some of the bony landmarks are colored dark blue on a bone that is colored light blue. This technique is used to help you recall that bony landmarks belong to bones. For example, if you look at the glabella, you will see that it is technically called the glabella of the frontal bone.

Spend time going through all the images here in this 2D anatomy menu.
