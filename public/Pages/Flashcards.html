Flashcards

Students have reported that digital flashcards have been one of the most useful tools to help accomplish the task of memorizing large numbers of anatomical structures. It is for this reason that we have created some practice digital flashcards. We use a program called Anki to do this

[CLICK HERE] to open a new page and learn more about Anki. You can also download the Anki program to your computer for free using this link. Anki does support apps on smart phones if that is the way you would prefer to use digital flashcards. However, smartphone apps may cost money while the computer versions are free.

Watch the short introductory video below and then notice that below the video is a link to a flashcard deck that you can download to begin using for more practice.

Just one word of caution! Some students will try to use only the flashcards to prepare for the exam. This rarely works out well. There will be items on the exams that you have never seen before. This is done on purpose because we believe that memorizing a picture of anatomy is not learning anatomy. However, we prefer that students really begin to understand anatomy and learn where it is in relation to other structures and how its shape and form might be patterned for recogniztion in any number of artist renditions or even a real cadaver. In other words, while the flashcards can help, we highly recommend that you do your own internet searches for anatomical structures that you are not sure you could recognize in other formats and be sure to do all the activities for each week in lab as they are geared to give you exposure to anatomy in both 2D and 3D. Memorizing a whole bunch of pictures on the flashcards just won't get you ready. However, using the flashcards as an ancillary learning tool to everything else you do to get the anatomy down is a great idea!
